Productivity & Learning

CDPR — Frontlab

Being productive, learning, and doing all of it in a good mood and without stress is possible — as long as you follow a few rules and stay aware of a few things.

1Learn to dissociate

It is hard, but try to stay as conscious as possible of the difference between:

  • where you can produce value, and
  • where you still have to learn — the part where it is not reasonable to expect yourself to produce value yet.

This distinction is essential to break down your time and your tasks correctly.

2Take a step back before diving in

Before getting nose-deep in your project, ask yourself these questions about the work in the CDPR team.

A. What types of data do you work on?

Datatype schemas — to be added.

B. What types of processes do you apply, and what skills do they require?

Process schema — acquisition, preprocessing, and analysis stages with the associated tools at each step.

Keep in mind that, in the end, everything comes down to:

  • querying data (easy once it is in BIDS and well formatted),
  • processing data,
  • visualizing and summarizing data.

C. If you don't have a skill yourself, how do you get it?

Within the team? Outside the team?

3Keep perspective

Academia is a highly selective environment. Work with passion — but stay grounded.

  • No ego. You are (very) brilliant, that is certain — but you are also certainly not the best.
